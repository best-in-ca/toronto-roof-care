Asphalt Shingle Brand Comparison

The major shingle brands available in Toronto, compared on quality, warranty, finish options, and where each one wins.

"Architectural asphalt shingles" sounds like one category. From the contractor side, it's five major brands and dozens of product lines within each. The choice meaningfully affects price, lifespan, and how the roof looks 15 years from now. This guide walks through what's actually different between the brands stocked by Toronto roofers.

The five major brands in Toronto

GAF

The largest residential shingle manufacturer in North America. Headlines:

  • Premium line: Timberline HDZ — the best-selling architectural shingle in Toronto. 30-year limited warranty
  • Premium designer: Camelot II, Glenwood, Grand Sequoia — slate or shake mimicry
  • Pricing: Mid-to-premium
  • Warranty: Best-in-class on Master Elite installer projects (lifetime, transferable)
  • Wind rating: Up to 130 mph on HDZ line
  • Toronto availability: Excellent — most installers stock

IKO

Canadian-owned, North-American manufactured. Particularly strong presence in the GTA market.

  • Premium line: Dynasty — heavier than typical architectural, excellent durability
  • Mid-range: Cambridge — the most-installed shingle in the GTA
  • Designer: Royal Estate, Crowne Slate — high-end slate-mimicking
  • Pricing: Slightly below GAF for equivalent quality
  • Warranty: 25-30 years limited, transferable
  • Wind rating: Up to 130 mph on Dynasty
  • Toronto availability: Excellent — Canadian distribution

Owens Corning

Strong product, slightly less common in Toronto than GAF or IKO.

  • Premium line: Duration — solid architectural shingle, good warranty
  • Designer: Berkshire, Devonshire — premium designer options
  • Pricing: Comparable to GAF
  • Warranty: Lifetime limited on Duration, 50-year on designer lines
  • Wind rating: Up to 130 mph
  • Toronto availability: Good but less stocked than GAF/IKO

CertainTeed

Strong reputation, established in the Toronto market.

  • Premium line: Landmark — solid mid-grade architectural
  • Designer: Presidential, Grand Manor, Carriage House — slate and shake mimicry
  • Pricing: Comparable to other premium brands
  • Warranty: Lifetime limited with 4-Star Master Shingle Applicator install
  • Wind rating: Up to 130 mph
  • Toronto availability: Good

Malarkey

Newer to the Toronto market but growing rapidly. Innovative formulation.

  • Premium line: Vista AR / Legacy — high-quality with rubber additives
  • Distinctive features: NEX (nexgen polymer-modified asphalt) provides better flexibility and longer life
  • Pricing: Mid-to-premium
  • Warranty: Limited lifetime, transferable
  • Wind rating: Up to 130 mph
  • Toronto availability: Less common but increasing

What's actually different between brands

Asphalt formulation

The base asphalt mix affects flexibility, UV resistance, and adhesion. Modern modified asphalt formulations (especially Malarkey's NEX) outperform traditional formulations on real-world durability. The visible differences show after year 10–15.

Granule retention

How well the protective ceramic granules stay adhered. Premium brands generally hold granules longer, which directly extends shingle life. Visible difference: granules in the gutters at year 7 vs year 12+.

Algae resistance

Most Toronto brands now offer AR (algae-resistant) lines with copper or zinc granules. Avoid streaking on north-facing slopes. Worth specifying — premium granules add minimal cost.

Warranty terms (the fine print)

Marketed "lifetime" warranties are limited and prorated. Real protection is in the first 10–15 years. Transferable warranties (matter at resale) require correct registration after installation. The installer registers; you receive the certificate.

Wind ratings

Most premium shingles now rated to 110–130 mph. Real-world wind damage above 100 mph is rare in Toronto. The rating matters less than installation quality — properly-installed mid-rated shingles outperform poorly-installed high-rated ones.

Colour and aesthetic

Each brand has dozens of colours. Cross-brand colours don't match exactly. Once you start with one brand, future repairs need to match within that brand's line. This locks you in somewhat.

Which brand for which project?

Mid-range Toronto detached home

GAF Timberline HDZ or IKO Cambridge. Either delivers 25–30 years with proper installation. Cost difference under $500 across the whole roof.

Premium GTA home (over $1.5M)

Designer line from any major brand — GAF Camelot II, IKO Royal Estate, CertainTeed Presidential. Slate or shake aesthetic without the cost of natural materials.

Heritage Toronto neighbourhood

Designer line specifying slate or shake appearance. CertainTeed Grand Manor or GAF Slateline often spec'd in heritage districts.

Maximum longevity

Malarkey Vista with NEX polymer formulation. Real-world 35+ year lifespan in Toronto conditions. Premium price but per-year cost lowest among asphalt options.

Budget-conscious rental property

Standard architectural — GAF Royal Sovereign, IKO Marathon. 20–25 years, lower cost, perfectly adequate for non-owner-occupied properties.

Beyond brand: what matters more

Installation quality outweighs brand choice. A poor installer with GAF Timberline produces a worse roof than a great installer with mid-grade IKO. Specifically:

  • Manufacturer-spec nailing (4 nails per shingle, in the right zone)
  • Ice-and-water shield extending past code minimum
  • Drip edge installed correctly
  • Proper attic ventilation (which affects all shingles equally)
  • Step flashing at all wall intersections
  • Manufacturer-certified installer status (GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed 4-Star, etc.)

The 2026 winner for most Toronto homes

For an average Toronto detached home replacing in 2026, the best value is one of:

  • GAF Timberline HDZ — most widely installed, easy warranty enforcement, good colour selection
  • IKO Cambridge or Dynasty — Canadian product, strong GTA distribution, competitive pricing
  • Malarkey Vista — best long-term durability, premium price
