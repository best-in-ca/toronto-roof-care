Toronto Roof Maintenance Calendar

A 12-month care guide. What to do each month, what to watch for, and the small actions that double the lifespan of your roof.

Get an inspection Seasonal care detail

Roof maintenance isn't a once-a-decade event. A Toronto roof lasts longer and performs better when you give it 10 minutes of attention each quarter and one professional visit each year. This calendar covers what to do each month — most of it is just looking up, but the looking matters.

Quarterly priorities

Q1 (Jan–Mar) — Winter monitoring

Watch from the ground. Look for ice dam buildup after thaws. Document any leaks immediately with photos. Don't climb on the roof in winter conditions. If active leaks appear, contact emergency repair for stabilization.

Q2 (Apr–Jun) — Spring assessment and repair

Walk the property and inspect from the ground. Schedule a professional inspection if last one was 18+ months ago. Address any winter damage before storm season. Book repair work now — May–June has the best contractor availability before peak summer.

Q3 (Jul–Sep) — Major work window

Best weather for replacements and major repairs. If your roof is approaching end-of-life or you've been deferring work, this quarter is the time. Toronto roofers are busiest in August–September — book ahead.

Q4 (Oct–Dec) — Pre-winter prep

The most important quarter for maintenance. Eavestrough cleanout, attic ventilation check, gutter and downspout inspection. Anything not addressed by November waits until spring. See the seasonal care guide for the detailed checklist.

What to look for each month

From the ground (anytime)

  • Missing or lifted shingles
  • Granules visible in gutters or at downspouts
  • Dark streaks on the roof surface
  • Sagging or dipping ridges
  • Visible water staining on soffits or fascia

In the eavestroughs (twice yearly)

  • Granule accumulation
  • Plant growth
  • Sagging gutter sections
  • Loose or detached downspouts

In the attic (annually)

  • Water staining on the deck
  • Mould or wood discoloration
  • Insulation condition and depth
  • Blocked soffit vents
  • Frost or condensation (winter check)

Inside the house (anytime)

  • New ceiling stains
  • Peeling paint on top floor
  • Drafts at top-floor windows or walls

The DIY vs professional split

You can do

  • Ground-level visual inspection
  • Eavestrough cleanout (with proper safety)
  • Photo documentation of damage
  • Interior ceiling and attic inspection

Call a professional for

  • Anything requiring being on the roof itself
  • Any flashing work
  • Any attic ventilation modifications
  • Any repair after the warranty period of the original install
  • Any insurance damage claim
  • Annual or biannual inspection (free from The Roof Technician)

Maintenance budget framework

A typical Toronto roof costs the following over its 25-year life:

  • Annual eavestrough cleanout: $150–300/year ($3,750–7,500 lifetime if paid)
  • Periodic professional inspections (every 3 years): $0 (most contractors offer free)
  • Minor repairs over 25 years: $1,500–3,000 cumulative
  • Major repair work (around year 15–20): $1,800–4,000
  • Eventual replacement: $9,000–15,000

Total cost of ownership: roughly $700–1,200/year averaged over the roof's life.

The roof you have, by age

  • 0–10 years: Watch and document. Minimal intervention needed
  • 10–15 years: Annual professional inspection becomes important. Address any flashing issues
  • 15–20 years: Budget for end-of-life. Major repairs may be worth it; major investments may not
  • 20+ years: Plan replacement within 2–4 years. Don't let it fail catastrophically. See roof replacement services
